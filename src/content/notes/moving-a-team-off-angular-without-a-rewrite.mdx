Every "we should switch frameworks" conversation dies the same way: someone says
"rewrite," everyone pictures a six-month freeze, and the idea is buried. Here's how
we moved off Angular without ever stopping shipping.

## Why the question even came up

What was actually hurting? Be specific — hiring, build times, the parts of Angular
the team kept fighting. Avoid "React is trendy."

## Selling the migration, not the rewrite

The pitch that worked: no big bang, no freeze. New surfaces in React, old ones left
alone until they were worth touching. How did you get buy-in from people who'd built
the Angular app?

## Making it boring on purpose

The de-risking moves — the seam between the two stacks, what you migrated first, how
you proved it before asking anyone to commit.

## Bringing the team with you

The human half. Pairing, the first PRs, the skeptic who became the advocate. This is
the part hiring managers actually read.
